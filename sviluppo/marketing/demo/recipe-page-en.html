Grandma's tiramisù: the original recipe

by Mary R. · 12 comments · 8 min read
[ dessert photo ]
Sponsored content

Tiramisù is one of the most loved desserts. In this recipe I show you how to make it the old-fashioned way, with a few genuine ingredients and some little family secrets.

Ad 300×250

Start by whisking the yolks with the sugar until pale and fluffy. Add the mascarpone a little at a time, folding gently so the mixture stays light.

Dip the ladyfingers in cold coffee, just for an instant: they should stay soft, not soggy. Layer ladyfingers and cream in a dish.

Finish with a generous dusting of cocoa. Chill for at least three hours before serving. The result? A creamy, unforgettable dessert.
