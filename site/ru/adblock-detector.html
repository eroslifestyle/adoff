    Blocking ads is not the same as being invisible

    There are two very different things an ad blocker can do. The first is blocking: stopping ads from loading or hiding them in the page. Almost every blocker does this. The second is staying invisible: making sure the website cannot tell that any blocking happened. Very few blockers do this.

    The test above runs the same kinds of probes that anti-adblock scripts use: it drops a decoy ad element into the page and checks if it gets hidden, and it tries to load known ad-network resources and checks if they get stopped. If your blocker reacts to these probes in a measurable way, a website can detect it — and that is exactly what triggers "please disable your ad blocker" walls.

    AdOff's Stealth mode takes the opposite approach. It blocks at the network and cosmetic level and spoofs the signals anti-adblock systems look for, so the probes come back looking completely normal. Ads are gone; the website sees nothing unusual.

    Why does my ad blocker show as "detectable"?

    Because it blocks ads in a visible way: it removes decoy elements and stops ad requests, and websites can measure both. That's normal for most blockers — it's the trade-off between blocking and being invisible. Stealth-capable blockers avoid leaving these traces.
