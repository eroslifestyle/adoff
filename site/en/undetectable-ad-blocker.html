How websites detect ad blockers

Anti-adblock systems plant ad bait elements and check if they get hidden, launch test requests to ad servers and check if they get blocked, and look for ad-related JavaScript variables. A normal blocker reacts to all this in a way the page can measure.

Once detected, the site can hide content behind a wall, show a pushy message or silently degrade your experience. You blocked the ads, but the site knows, and it reacts.

What makes a blocker undetectable

Being undetectable means passing those probes as if no blocker was present: the bait element seems visible, test requests seem to succeed, expected variables exist. AdOff operates in the page's main execution context and fakes the bait elements, intercepts detection fetches and masks the variables that anti-adblock relies on.

The result: ads get removed at the network and cosmetic level, but the probes come back clean. No wall, no messages.
