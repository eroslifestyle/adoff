The honesty test: what do they actually log?

This is where things get interesting. Both AdGuard and AdOff claim "no-log" VPN policies. Let's look at what each actually discloses:

AdGuard VPN — what's disclosed

AdGuard's privacy policy discloses that it collects:

  • Connection timestamps (anonymized, without IP)
  • Data volume used per session
  • Technical diagnostic data

This is reasonable for a freemium VPN (free tier needs to prevent abuse). But the transparency varies between the free and paid tiers, and it's not always crystal clear which data applies to paying customers.

AdOff Premium VPN — what's disclosed

AdOff publishes a dedicated VPN Policy that makes an explicit, unambiguous list:

  • Only account status (active/disabled)
  • Only last connection timestamp (for 7-day auto-disable)
  • No browsing history, no traffic logs, no IP addresses, no data volume

The explicit VPN Policy (separate from the general Privacy Policy) is unusual in the industry. It's a deliberate choice to over-communicate rather than hide behind generic "no-log" language.

Winner on transparency: AdOff Premium. The dedicated VPN Policy document, with an explicit list of what is and is NOT logged, sets a higher standard than the typical "no-log" boilerplate.

The anti-adblock wall problem

This is where the comparison gets decisive. More and more websites — YouTube, news sites, forums — detect and block ad blocker users with increasingly sophisticated techniques.

Neither AdGuard nor AdOff Premium can promise to bypass every anti-adblock wall. But:

AdOff Premium: Stealth Mode

AdOff's Stealth Mode (included in Premium, also in Pro/Trial) operates in the browser's MAIN world and performs:

  • Variable spoofing: Masquerades the browser's detected environment to look like a normal user
  • Fetch/XHR interception: Intercepts test requests that sites send to detect ad blockers
  • Bait element simulation: Creates the DOM elements ad blocker detectors look for

Stealth Mode does not guarantee universal bypass — determined adversaries can always evolve — but it's the most technically sophisticated approach among consumer ad blockers.

AdGuard: Stealth (different feature)

AdGuard has a "Stealth Mode" in its settings, but it refers to anti-tracking features — removing tracking parameters from URLs, blocking WebRTC, hiding user agent, and similar. It does not spoof the browser environment to bypass anti-adblock walls.

Important: "Stealth Mode" means different things at AdGuard vs AdOff. AdGuard's Stealth Mode hides you from trackers. AdOff's Stealth Mode hides the fact that you have an ad blocker installed. Choose based on what you need.
