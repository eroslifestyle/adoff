The fastest way: install an ad blocker

Chrome doesn't block ads on its own. You add the capability with an extension. Here's the whole process:

  1. Open the Chrome Web Store and search for your ad blocker (or go straight to the developer's install page).
  2. Click Add to Chrome, then confirm.
  3. Pin the extension (puzzle icon → pin) so you can pause it per-site when you want to support a creator.
  4. That's it. Reload any page and the ads are gone.

What "blocking all ads" actually requires

Most people are surprised that ad blockers vary a lot in what they block. Truly removing every ad takes three different layers working together:

  • Network blocking — stops ad and tracker requests before they load. This is the foundation and the biggest speed boost.
  • Cosmetic filtering — hides leftover ad slots and empty containers in the page so you don't see blank gaps.
  • Video ad neutralization — the hardest part. Streaming players inject ads at the SDK level, so a blocker has to neutralize the ad SDK itself, not just block a URL.

Free, basic blockers usually do the first two. Video ads and anti-adblock walls are where most of them fall short.

The problem nobody warns you about: detection

Blocking ads is only half the battle. Many websites run anti-adblock scripts that detect your blocker and then hide content or nag you to turn it off. A normal blocker blocks ads in a visible way, so these scripts catch it easily.

The fix is a stealth layer that blocks ads and hides the fact that any blocking happened. You can test whether your current ad blocker is detectable in your browser, privately, in five seconds.
